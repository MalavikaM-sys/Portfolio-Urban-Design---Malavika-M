Project 01
Rydalmere Waterfront
Urban Design Studio: Urban Precinct (ARCH9001)  ·  Team of 4  ·  2024  ·  Guide: Prof. Tanya Vincent
Aim of the Project
To envision the development of the Rydalmere Precinct in response to the upcoming proposal for Stage 2 of the Parramatta Light Rail project, using the opportunity to activate the waterfront along the precinct's industrial and residential edges.
Regional Context
Rydalmere sits along the Parramatta River corridor, linked to Parramatta and Sydney Olympic Park by light rail, ferry and bus routes.
Key Moves — Site & Context
01. Establish NORTH – SOUTH CONNECTIONS for pedestrian, cycling and vehicular movement
02. BUILT EDGE ACTIVATION along the water edge resulting in better edges facing the water
03. ACTIVE COMMUNITY AND RETAIL CENTER and complimentary street edges responding to the same
04. RETAINING majority of the LOCAL JOBS AND ECONOMY on the site in addition to introduction of new economic opportunities
05. RESPONDING TO THE NATURAL FEATURES of the site to design street and built form to respond and interact with existing elements
06. Utilising SITE TOPOGRAPHY to DICTATE BUILT FORM heights with the density tapering towards the site
→
Vision
This proposal for Rydalmere envisions a vibrant waterfront community that connects people, supports diverse local economies, and celebrates natural landscapes, fostering a thriving, resilient public realm for all, with the exploration of the potential to incorporate Connecting with Country strategies, deepening the site's cultural and environmental connections.
Built Form
A transit-oriented waterfront precinct that steps building form down toward the river, framing active street edges and public space along the way.
